A will is essential, but it is only one piece of the puzzle. What happens if you become seriously ill or injured before you pass away? Who makes financial decisions on your behalf? Who talks to your doctors? Who has access to your medical records?

Our Will + Estate Plan Bundle answers all of these questions. Everything You Need

What's Included in the Bundle

Five essential documents that work together to protect you and your family.

Last Will & Testament

Name your beneficiaries, designate a guardian for minor children, choose your executor, and control how your property is distributed. Includes self-proving affidavit.

Durable Power of Attorney

Appoint someone you trust to manage your finances, pay bills, handle banking, and make business decisions if you become incapacitated. This document can prevent the need for a costly court-supervised guardianship.

Medical Power of Attorney

Designate the person who will make healthcare decisions on your behalf when you cannot communicate. Without this, your family may face delays and disputes at the worst possible time.

HIPAA Authorization

Gives your designated agents the legal right to access your medical records and communicate with your healthcare providers. Without this, hospitals may refuse to share information -- even with your spouse.

Directive to Physicians

Also known as a "living will," this document states your wishes about life-sustaining treatment if you are terminally ill or in an irreversible condition. It removes the burden of that decision from your family's shoulders.

Execution & Organization

All documents are properly executed with witnesses and notarization. You receive an organized estate planning binder with originals and copies, plus digital backups.

Is This Right for You?

Who Needs the Estate Plan Bundle?

Every Adult Over 18

Accidents and illness do not wait until you are old. If you are an adult with any assets, relationships, or responsibilities, you need this protection.

Parents of Young Children

Name guardians for your children and ensure that if something happens to both parents, the right people are legally empowered to step in immediately.

Aging Parents & Their Children

If your parents do not have powers of attorney in place, a sudden health crisis could leave your family unable to manage their care or finances without going to court.

Business Owners

If you are incapacitated, who signs checks, manages payroll, and keeps your business running? A durable POA can keep your business alive while you recover.

Married Couples

Even spouses do not automatically have the right to make all financial and medical decisions for each other. These documents fill that gap.

Anyone Upgrading from a Will-Only Plan

If you already have a will but nothing else, this bundle fills the critical gaps in your plan and gives your family complete protection.

A will only takes effect after you pass away. It tells the court who gets your property and who cares for your children. Having both means every area of your life is covered.
