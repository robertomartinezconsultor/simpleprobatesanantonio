Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the questions we hear most from families navigating the probate process in Texas.

At Joe's Probate Services, uncontested probate starts at $2,500 as a flat fee. This includes attorney fees, document preparation, and court filings. Court filing fees (typically $300–$400 in Bexar County) are additional. We never charge hidden fees or hourly rates for standard uncontested probate cases. During your free consultation, we'll give you an exact quote based on your specific situation.

Most uncontested probate cases in Bexar County take 3 to 6 months from filing to completion. A Muniment of Title can be completed in as little as 6–8 weeks. The timeline depends on the complexity of the estate, court scheduling, and whether all heirs are cooperative. We work proactively to keep your case moving and avoid unnecessary delays.

In most cases, yes. Even with a valid will, Texas law requires the will to be admitted to probate court before assets can be legally transferred. However, some assets with named beneficiaries—like life insurance policies, retirement accounts, and payable-on-death bank accounts—pass outside of probate. We offer a free consultation to determine exactly what your situation requires.

A Muniment of Title is a simplified probate process that transfers property directly under the will without appointing an executor. It's faster and cheaper but is only available when there are no unpaid debts (other than debts secured by real property). Independent Administration appoints a personal representative (executor) who has the authority to manage estate assets, pay debts, and distribute property with minimal court oversight. During your consultation, we'll help you determine which option is right for your situation.

Yes, there are several ways to avoid probate in Texas. These include setting up a revocable living trust, using transfer-on-death designations for vehicles and real estate (Lady Bird deeds or TOD deeds), naming beneficiaries on financial accounts, and using payable-on-death designations. Our estate planning services can help you structure your assets to minimize or eliminate the need for probate entirely. The best time to plan is now—schedule a free consultation to learn more.
