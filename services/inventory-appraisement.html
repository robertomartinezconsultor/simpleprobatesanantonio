Inventory, Appraisement & List of Claims

Home  /  Services  /  Inventory & Appraisement

Court-Required Document

The Complete Picture of Your Loved One's Estate

If you've been appointed as an executor or administrator of an estate in Texas, one of your most important responsibilities is filing an Inventory, Appraisement, and List of Claims with the probate court. This document is essentially a complete snapshot of the estate — everything your loved one owned and everything they owed at the time of their passing.

Texas law requires this document to be filed within 90 days of being appointed as the estate's representative. It must list all assets (real estate, bank accounts, investments, vehicles, personal property), their estimated values, and all known debts and claims against the estate.

We know this can feel overwhelming — especially when you're still grieving and juggling the many other responsibilities that come with managing an estate. The good news is that you don't have to figure it out alone. We work with you to identify all the assets and debts, organize the information in the format the court requires, and file it on time.

Getting this document right matters. It protects you as the executor from personal liability, it ensures beneficiaries know exactly what's in the estate, and it satisfies the court's requirements so your probate case stays on track.

Whether you're working with another attorney on the broader probate case and just need help with the inventory, or you handled the probate filing yourself and now need this next step completed — we're here to help.
