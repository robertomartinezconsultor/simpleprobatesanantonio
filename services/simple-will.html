Every Family Deserves a Will

A will is the most important legal document you will ever sign. It tells the world who should receive your property, who should care for your children, and how your final wishes should be honored. Without one, the State of Texas decides all of that for you -- and the state does not know your family the way you do.

At Joe's Probate, we believe that creating a will should not be complicated, intimidating, or expensive. Our Simple Will Package gives you a professionally drafted, legally binding will that is customized to your family's needs. We walk you through the process step by step, in plain English (or Spanish), so you always know exactly what your documents say and why they matter.

Whether you are a young parent wanting to name a guardian for your children, a homeowner looking to pass your property to your spouse, or a grandparent making sure your grandchildren are provided for, our Simple Will Package is designed for you.

What Happens If You Die Without a Will in Texas?

In Texas, dying without a will is called dying "intestate." When this happens, the state's default inheritance laws decide who gets your property. That might sound harmless, but the results can be shocking. If you are married with children from a previous relationship, your surviving spouse may not receive the home you shared. If you are unmarried, distant relatives you have never met may inherit everything you worked for. And if you have minor children, a judge -- not you -- will decide who raises them.

A will gives you control. It puts your wishes in writing so your family does not have to guess, argue, or spend thousands of dollars in court trying to figure out what you would have wanted.

Is This Right for You?

Who Needs a Simple Will?

Parents with Minor Children

You want to name a guardian and make sure your children are cared for by someone you trust -- not someone a judge picks.

Married Couples

You want to make sure your spouse inherits your property cleanly and quickly, without complications from Texas intestacy laws.

Homeowners

If you own a home, a car, or any significant assets, a will makes sure they go where you want them to go.

Blended Families

Texas intestacy laws can produce unexpected results for blended families. A will lets you provide for stepchildren and your spouse the way you intend.

Single Adults

Without a will, your parents or siblings inherit everything. If that is not what you want, a will lets you choose your own beneficiaries.

First-Time Planners

If you have never had a will before, this is the perfect starting point. It is simple, affordable, and gives your family real protection.
