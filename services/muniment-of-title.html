Probate of Will as Muniment of Title

Home  /  Services  /  Muniment of Title

Fastest Probate in Texas

The Simplest Way to Honor Your Loved One's Wishes

When someone you love passes away and leaves a will, you want to make sure their wishes are carried out as simply and quickly as possible. A Muniment of Title is the fastest and most affordable way to do exactly that in Texas.

Unlike other types of probate, this process doesn't require appointing an executor or administrator through the court. There's no need for ongoing court supervision, no complicated reporting requirements, and no drawn-out timeline. In many cases, the entire process can be completed in as little as 30 days.

Think of it this way: the court simply validates the will and recognizes it as the legal document that transfers property to the people named in it. That's it. No administrator standing between your family and the assets your loved one wanted them to have.

This is especially common here in San Antonio and throughout Bexar County, where families often need to transfer a home, bank accounts, or vehicles after a loss. If your loved one left a valid will and there are no significant unpaid debts (other than debts secured by real estate, like a mortgage), Muniment of Title is almost always the right choice.

We handle the entire process for you — from preparing the application to attending the court hearing on your behalf — so you can focus on your family during this difficult time.

Is This Right for You?

Who Needs Muniment of Title?

Your Loved One Left a Will

The deceased had a valid Texas will (or a will valid in the state where it was signed) and you have access to the original document.

No Significant Unpaid Debts

There are no outstanding debts that need to be paid from the estate — other than debts secured by real property, like a mortgage or home equity loan.

Need to Transfer Property

You need to transfer a house, land, bank accounts, vehicles, or other assets into the names of the people listed in the will.

Want the Fastest Process

You want to resolve the estate as quickly and affordably as possible, without ongoing court involvement or administrator duties.
