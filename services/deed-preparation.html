Real Property Transfer

Transfer Your Home Without Probate

For most San Antonio families, their home is their most valuable asset. But if your home is titled only in your name when you pass away, your family will likely need to go through probate just to transfer ownership -- even if your will says exactly who should get it. That process costs money, takes time, and adds stress during an already difficult period.

The good news is that Texas offers special types of deeds that let you transfer your home to your loved ones automatically when you pass away, without probate and without giving up any control while you are alive. These deeds are simple, affordable, and incredibly effective.

Transfer on Death Deed (TODD)

A Transfer on Death Deed (TODD) lets you name a beneficiary for your home. While you are alive, the deed has no effect -- you keep full ownership, you can sell the property, refinance it, or revoke the deed at any time. But when you pass away, the property automatically transfers to your named beneficiary without probate. It is one of the simplest and most cost-effective estate planning tools available in Texas.

Lady Bird Deed (Enhanced Life Estate Deed)

A Lady Bird Deed (also called an Enhanced Life Estate Deed) works similarly to a TODD but with some additional benefits. You retain a "life estate" in the property, meaning you keep full control during your lifetime -- including the right to sell, mortgage, or lease the property without your beneficiary's permission. At your death, the property passes automatically to your named beneficiary. Lady Bird Deeds are particularly popular for Medicaid planning because the property generally does not count as a transfer that would trigger a Medicaid penalty period.

Which Deed Is Right for You?

Both deeds accomplish the goal of transferring your home without probate, but they have different technical features that matter depending on your situation. During your consultation, we will explain the differences, review your specific circumstances, and recommend the best option for you and your family.

Is This Right for You?

Who Needs Deed Preparation?

Homeowners Who Want to Avoid Probate

If your home is your primary asset and you want it to pass directly to your children or spouse without court involvement, a TODD or Lady Bird Deed is the simplest solution.

Aging Parents Passing Property to Children

Many older parents want to make sure the family home goes to their children seamlessly. These deeds accomplish that goal without giving up any control during your lifetime.

Families Concerned About Medicaid

A Lady Bird Deed can protect your home from Medicaid estate recovery while allowing you to qualify for benefits. It is a key tool in Medicaid planning.

Budget-Conscious Planners

If a full living trust is not in your budget right now, a TODD or Lady Bird Deed can protect your biggest asset (your home) for a fraction of the cost.

Property Owners Adding to Their Estate Plan

Even if you already have a will or trust, a deed can provide an extra layer of protection and ensure your home transfers without any delay or court involvement.

Single Property Owners

If you are single and own a home, without a deed or trust your property could end up in probate. A simple TODD solves this problem quickly and affordably.
