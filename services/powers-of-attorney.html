Incapacity Planning

Make Sure the Right Person Speaks for You

Imagine this: you are in the hospital after a serious accident. You cannot speak, you cannot sign documents, you cannot make decisions. Your family is terrified. Your spouse tries to access your bank account to pay the mortgage -- denied. Your adult child tries to get information from your doctor -- denied. Your bills pile up. Your business suffers. And the only way your family can get the legal authority to help you is to go through a court-supervised guardianship that takes months and costs thousands.

This happens every day to families in San Antonio. And it is completely preventable. A Powers of Attorney package puts the right people in charge of your financial and medical decisions before a crisis hits, so your family can act immediately when you need them most.

Our standalone Powers of Attorney package is perfect for anyone who already has a will or trust but does not yet have incapacity planning documents in place. It is also ideal for young adults, college students heading out of state, and aging parents who need to make sure their children can help them when the time comes.

Why Every Adult Needs These Documents

Once you turn 18, your parents no longer have the legal right to make decisions for you -- even in an emergency. If a college student is in an accident, their parents cannot access medical records or make treatment decisions without a Medical Power of Attorney and HIPAA Authorization. The same is true for spouses: marriage does not automatically give you authority over your partner's finances or full access to their medical information. These documents fill that gap.

Complete Package

What's Included

Four essential documents that protect your decisions and your family.

Durable Power of Attorney

Appoint a trusted agent to manage your financial affairs -- banking, bill-paying, real estate, investments, and tax matters -- if you become incapacitated. "Durable" means it remains effective even after you lose mental capacity.

Medical Power of Attorney

Name the person you trust to make healthcare decisions when you are unable to communicate. They can consent to treatment, choose doctors, approve surgery, and make end-of-life decisions on your behalf.

HIPAA Authorization

Without this document, federal privacy laws prevent hospitals and doctors from sharing your medical information with anyone -- even your spouse or children. This authorization gives your designated agents the legal right to access your records and communicate with your care team.

Directive to Physicians

Also called a "living will," this document states your wishes about life-sustaining treatment if you are terminally ill or in an irreversible condition. It relieves your family of the burden of guessing what you would have wanted.

Proper Execution & Copies

All documents are properly executed with witnesses and notarization per Texas requirements. You receive originals for safekeeping and copies for your agents and healthcare providers.

Is This Right for You?

Who Needs Powers of Attorney?

Every Adult Over 18

Once you turn 18, no one -- not even your parents or spouse -- has automatic legal authority to make decisions for you. These documents give that authority to the people you choose.

College Students Leaving Home

If your child is heading to college, these documents ensure you can still access their medical information and make decisions in an emergency. This is one of the most overlooked preparations for new college families.

Aging Parents

If your parents do not have powers of attorney, a sudden health crisis could force your family into a costly, time-consuming guardianship proceeding. Getting these documents in place now can save your family enormous stress later.

People with Existing Wills

If you already have a will but no incapacity planning documents, this package fills the gap. A will only takes effect after death -- these documents protect you while you are alive.

Business Owners

If you are incapacitated, who signs the checks? Who manages payroll? A durable POA can authorize someone you trust to keep your business running while you recover.

Frequent Travelers

If you travel frequently for work or pleasure, having someone authorized to handle your affairs in your absence provides important peace of mind.
